Jewish History

3760 BCE
In The Beginning

The world was created along with Adam and Chavah.

3526 BCE
Adam's Grandson

Enosh is born.

Lyon Cathedral stained glass window, completed 1496

3500 BCE
Wheels

The wheel is first used in Mesopotamia,modern day Iraq.

This Ljubljana Marshes Wheel with axle is the oldest wooden wheel yet discovered dating to Copper Age (c. 3,130 BCE)

3400 BCE
Egyptians

First Egyptian hieroglyphs appear.

Bone and ivory tags, pottery vessels, and clay seal impressions bearing hieroglyphs unearthed at Abydos, 300 miles south of Cairo, have been dated to between 3400 and 3200 B.C., making them the oldest known examples of Egyptian writing.

3114 BCE
Mayans

Start of the Mayan calendar. The Mayans had 20 days in their month starting with day 0 and ending with day 19. They understood zero not only as a place holder, but as a true counting number.

El Castillo, also known as the Temple of Kukulcan is a Mesoamerican step-pyramid that dominates the center of the Chichen Itza archaeological site in the Mexican state of Yucatán. The temple building is more formally designated by archaeologists as Chichen Itza.

3100 BCE
Stonehenge

Work begins on Stonehenge in England. Some of the stones came from 240 miles away, the Preseli Mountains in southwestern Wales. What possessed the Neolithic people to build such a monument is still unknown.

It has 93 stones, of which 30 are the upright sarsens that form the ring. The sarsens are 13 feet high, 7 feet wide, and weigh 25 tons each.

3074 BCE
Oldest Human

Metushelach (son of Chanoch) was born, living 969 years,Methuselah also alludes to having the ineffable name of G‑d engraved upon his sword.

Stained glass window of Methuselah from the southwest transept of Canterbury Cathedral in Kent, England. The Cathedral was built in 597 and went through massive renovations in 1075.

2705 BCE
Noah is Born

Only 600 years later will Noah save the world.

2700 BCE
Modern Calendar is Created

Egyptians create the 365 day calendar with the new year starting in June.

“The Egyptians were the first of all men who discovered the year, and they said that they found this from the stars.” Herodotus Histories II-4 (died 425 BC)

2560 BCE
Pyramids

The Great Pyramid at Giza in Egypt is finished. It will be the tallest man-made structure for 3,800 years only being eclipsed by St. Paul's Cathedral in 1240.

It took about 20 years for the Great Pyramid to be completed, The Greek historian Herodotus posited that the work required 100,000 laborers who were divided into five clans of 20,000 men each. However, recent evidence shows that the work required a workforce of 14,500-40,000 men.

2225 BCE
Ark

Noah began the construction of the ark

Allegedly, a reliquary displaying a piece of wood at the museum of Etchmiadzin Cathedral in Armenia said to be from Noah's Ark, By tradition, Jacob of Nisibis (died 350 AD) received the wood from an angel during his search for the Ark.

2105 BCE
Flood

The great flood covered the earth.

2000 BCE
Pi

The Babylonians estimate pi to be 3.125 using a hexagon inscribed in a circle.

1813 BCE
Forefathers

Abraham son of terach is born, Sarah is born 10 years later.

1765 BCE
Tower of Babel

The peoples of Babel are dispersed after the construction of the Tower.

1738 BCE
Israel

Abraham settled in Cannan. The land which G-d promised to Abraham's descendants through his son Isaac and grandson Jacob. Later called the Land of Israel, at the time of G-d's promise it was inhabited by the descendants of Canaan, son of Ham and grandson of Noah, who took the land by force from the children of Shem.

1713 BCE
Apocalypse

Sodom and Gomorrah are destroyed, Issac son of Abraham is born.

1677 BCE
End of the Beginning

Sarah dies as well as Abraham 39 years later.

1590 BCE
Lentil Soup

Isaac blessed Jacob instead of Esau.

1569 BCE
Tricked

Jacob married Leah and Rachel.

King of Egypt

Joseph became viceroy of Egypt after being sold into slavery by his brothers.

1506 BCE
Jacob Dies

Jacob dies.

Beginning of the End

After Levi died, the enslavement in Egypt began.

1393 BCE
New Beginnings

Moses is born.

1314 BCE
Burning Bush

Moses meets with G-d for the first time beside a burning bush.

1313 BCE
A New Nation

The Jews leave Egypt, cross the Red Sea, and recieve the Torah at Mount Sinai.

1258 BCE
A Lasting Home

After seven years of continuous war, Joshua divided the conquered land of Isreal among the different tribes. Reuben, Gad, and half of the tribe of Manasseh had already received their portion on the other side of the Jordan. Levi received no share. Instead, the Levites were given forty-eight cities throughout the land of Israel, among them, the "cities of refuge," where an unintentional killer could find refuge. Attached to the service of the Holy Temple, the Levites became the teachers of Israel..

1228 BCE
Judges

The rule of Judges continues for 297 years

1200 BCE
Iron Age

The Iron Age is the final epoch of the three historical Metal Ages, after the Copper and Bronze Ages.

1185 BCE
Greeks vs. Troy

The Greeks and Ilium (another name for Troy, hence "The Iliad") square off in the Trojan War.

907 BCE
Davidic Dynasty

Dovid is born.

869 BCE
King

Dovid becomes king of Israel in Jerusalem.

827 BCE
1/3

The first Beit Hamikdash was completed.

797 BCE
Division

Solomon died and his kingdom was divided

775 BCE
Homer

Greece starts to recover from the Bronze Age collapse. The blind poet Homer recites the Iliad and the Odyssey. This is written down since the art of writing had been rediscovered.

753 BCE
Rome

According to legend, Rome is founded by Romulus, grandson of King Numitor of Alba Longa, and son of Mars. Twelve birds circled overhead during the founding ceremonies and legend had it that the city would survive for 12 centuries.

550 BCE
Ancient Feat

The Greek engineer Eupalinos designs a water tunnel 1036 meters long through a solid limestone mountain to bring water to the ancient city of Samos. Work started on both ends and met in the middle, an extraordinary feat of ancient mentioned by Herodotus. The tunnel was used for 1,100 years.

479 BCE
Plataea

110,000 Greek hoplites defeat 300,000 Persians at the battle of Plataea, The Persians suffered 257,000 casualties, the Greek only 159.

425 BCE
Besieged

The final Babylonian siege of Jerusalem, 2 years later the sacrifices ceased in the Temple, and it was destroyed.

372 BCE
Writing On The Wall

Prophet Daniel reads the writing on the wall and is thrown into the lion's den. A year later, Zerubabel led the return to Israel, a year after that the construction of the second Temple began but was halted.

357 BCE
Purim

Esther took action against Haman's decree, 2 years later Mordechai proclaims the celebration of Purim

349 BCE
2/3

The second Beit Hamikdash was completed.

333 BCE
Alexander

Alexander the Great defeats Persia under Darius at battle of Issus. Alexander was the fourth in a line of great men and scholars: Socrates, Plato, Aristotle. Darius escapes and gathers his forces for the next battle. Alexander conqueres Egypt the following year.

313 BCE
Ezra Dies

Shimon the Tzaddik meets Alexander the Great.

139 BCE
Maccabbee

Yehuda the Maccabbe ruled, the 2nd Temple was rededicated, a year later Hannukah was declared a festival.

73 BCE
Spartacus

Spartacus, a former Roman soldier and gladiator fights against Rome.

44 BCE
Et tu?

A group of senators assassinate Julius Caesar on the Ides of March. They feared Caesar would be a king. After his death, his nephew Octavian organizes Caesar's troops, defeats those senators, and becomes Rome's first emperor..

11 BCE
Herod

Herod I finished rebuilding the second Beit Hamikdash

69 AD
Destruction

After a six month siege the Romans under the direction of Titus destroy Jerusalem killing one and a half million Jews. The gold taken from the the Temple finances the Colosseum back in Rome.

79 AD
Pompeii

Mount Vesuvius erupts and buries the cities of Pompeii and Herculaneum in ash preserving for modern scholars a window into ancient Roman life.

97 AD
China

Chinese General Pan Chao sends an embassy to the Roman empire.

133 AD
Betar

The Bar Kokhba revolt in Jerusalem against the Romans is finally crushed after two and a half years during which the Jewish state had home rule and a resumption of animal sacrifices. 580,000 Jews are killed in the restoration of Roman rule and the city of Jerusalem is reduced to rubble. Emperor Hadrian does not allow Jews to return to Jerusalem and renames the city Aelia Capitolina. The Jews will not have political control of Israel again for 1,813 years.

189 AD

Rabbi Yehuda haNasi completes the Mishnah.

Gemarah

Reb Ashi dies after completing the Gemarah.

550 AD
Green Energy

The first windmills are used by the Persians to power irrigation pumps, Mohammad is born 20 years later.

904 AD
Gunpowder

Gunpowder is used as a weapon for the first time in the siege of Wuchang, China. Although the Chinese developed gunpowder by mixing 75 percent saltpeter, 15 percent charcoal, and 10 percent sulfur centuries earlier for fireworks, this is the first recorded time to be used in a large battle.

1099 AD
Crusades

The Crusaders capture Jerusalem.

1105 AD
Rashi Dies

The Rambam is born, 9 years later the first recorded blood libel took place.

1149 AD
Oxford

1191 AD
Radak

Rabbi David Kimchi, known as the "Radak", wrote his commentary

1281 AD
Kamikaze

After conquering most of Asia, Kublai Khan invades Japan with 4,400 ships and 140,000 soldiers, but a Typhoon, a "Divine wind", (Kamikaze) destroys most of the fleet. 70,000 troops die in the storm - the worst naval disaster in history.

1290 AD
Expulsion

All Jews are expelled from England, 16 years later, from France as well.

1349 AD
Black Death

The Black Death massacres swept across Europe, as well as normal death from the plague that killed 50 million people or 50% of the European population at that time.

1481 AD

The Inquisition begins, 11 years later Jews are expelled from Spain and Sicily, coincidentally Christopher Columbus sails that year to Asia, and accidentally discovers the Americas.

1493 AD

Elizabeth Tudor, after surviving many attempts on her life, is crowned queen of England ushering in the English Renaissance where England becomes a major world power.
